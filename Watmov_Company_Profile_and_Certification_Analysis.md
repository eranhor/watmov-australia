## PART A — COMPANY PROFILE

### A.1 Who Watmov Is

**Watmov® Australia** is the Australian-facing arm of Watmov®, an established engineering design and drafting firm with a large production team based in **Pune, India**. Watmov positions itself as an **"extended design & drafting partner"** — an onshore-managed, offshore-delivered support team that lets Australian consultants, contractors and designers expand output, compress timelines and reduce cost without growing in-house headcount.

The model is deliberately **partnership-based, not "throw it over the fence."** Watmov aims to become an extension of the client's own drafting function: aligning to each client's output formats, title blocks, colours, logos and quality expectations, and to the regulatory/specification context the client works within (council specs, jurisdictional standards, etc.).

### A.3 Engagement Workflow

1. Client sends inquiry / project inputs to **info@watmov.com.au** (commercial) and/or **design@watmov.com.au** (design team).
2. Design team reviews inputs, clarifies, then returns a **proposal: effort level, budget, timeline**.
3. On confirmation, the design team owns technical communication through to close-out (drafts → review → modifications → final).
4. Over repeated projects Watmov internalises the client's formats and preferences, improving speed and consistency.

### A.4 Two Commercial Models

1. **Project-by-project** — client sends a project; Watmov scopes and quotes per project (priced on estimated **hours/effort**, not a flat rate, because complexity varies). Pricing is competitive but quality-led; payment terms can be flexible.
2. **Dedicated resource (FTE / part-FTE)** — client effectively "rents" a drafter (e.g. half a person) who works to the client's direction, still backed by Watmov's full engineering bench. (Comparable to the Philippines-style offshore-FTE model Australian firms already use, but with engineering depth behind it.)

> Typical entry path: try a few projects → if comfortable, move to a dedicated resource. Either entry point is supported.

## PART B — AUSTRALIAN CERTIFICATION & REGULATORY ANALYSIS

### B.1 The Governing Principle

In Australia, the regulated act is generally **taking professional/legal responsibility for a design — i.e. signing, certifying, declaring compliance, or submitting it to an authority** — *not* the act of drafting/producing the drawing itself. An offshore drafter can almost always **produce** the design; the question is **who carries the registered responsibility** when it reaches an Australian authority.

**Two exemptions recur across the regimes and are central to Watmov's risk position:**
- **"Direction & oversight" exemption** — work done under the direction and oversight of a registered/licensed Australian professional who takes responsibility for it is generally exempt from the offshore party needing registration.
- **"Prescriptive standard" exemption** — work done strictly to a prescriptive standard is generally exempt.

⚠️ **Critical ACT-specific change:** The **Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT)** introduced a mandatory **Professional Engineers Registration Scheme** (civil, structural, mechanical, electrical, fire safety). It commenced 6 Mar 2024 and registration became **mandatory from 6 March 2025**. Crucially, it **expressly captures services provided from outside the ACT for ACT projects.** This directly affects Watmov because EnvitoACT is in the ACT. *(Source: ACT Planning – Professional Engineers Registration Scheme; Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT); CMTEDD media release.)*

> The scheme is **not** triggered where the service is (a) under the direction & oversight of a registered professional engineer who takes responsibility, **or** (b) provided in accordance with a prescriptive standard. **These two exemptions are Watmov's compliance pathway.**

### B.2 Classification of Watmov Offerings for the Australian / ACT Market

#### 🟢 GREEN — No Australian certification required (low risk)

These are drafting/production and standards-driven tasks. The Australian client retains design responsibility and submits/certifies.

| Offering | Why it's clear |
|---|---|
| **General CAD drafting & detailing** (AutoCAD/Civil 3D/Revit) | Drafting is not a licensed occupation in Australia. |
| **PDF→CAD conversion, KMZ/Google-Earth basing, survey-file → plan** | Pure production/data processing. |
| **Bills of Quantities / material take-offs** | Quantity work, no statutory certification. |
| **Works-As-Executed / as-built drawings** from client GPS/field data | Record drafting; the licensed installer certifies the as-built record itself. |
| **Irrigation design** (sprinkler/drip, ovals, parks, medians, landscape) | No statutory licence to *produce* irrigation designs. Irrigation Australia's **Certified Irrigation Designer (CID)** is **voluntary** industry recognition, not a legal requirement. *(Source: Irrigation Australia – "A voluntary, national program of industry recognition.")* |
| **Installation/detail drawings, sheet-set assembly, title blocks, spec documents** | Production + documentation. |
| **Design to a prescriptive standard** (e.g. drawing strictly to ACT MITS 09D / MIS 18 Irrigation, AS/NZS 3500 layouts, council standard drawings) | Falls under the **prescriptive-standard exemption**. *(Source: ACT MITS/MIS; ACT PE Scheme guidance.)* |

#### 🟡 AMBER — Permissible but must flow through an Australian licensed/registered professional (manage deliberately)

Watmov can do the technical work, **but an appropriately registered/licensed Australian must take responsibility (sign/certify/declare) and provide direction & oversight.** Treat the Australian principal's sign-off as a contractual prerequisite.

| Offering | The constraint | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Plumbing & sanitary drainage / sewer plans (ACT)** | In the ACT, plumbing/drainage plans for commercial work (and water services ≥50 mm) **must be certified by a licensed *Plumbing Plan Certifier*** and registered with Access Canberra; plans must be designed to **AS/NZS 3500**. Watmov may **draft** them; a licensed ACT certifier **must certify**. EnvitoACT currently outsources certification — and is *considering becoming accredited himself*. **Watmov should draft "for certification by others."** | Construction Occupations (Licensing) Act 2004 (ACT); Water & Sewerage Act 2000 (ACT) s8; Access Canberra Plumber Note 31. |
| **Civil / hydraulic engineering for ACT projects** (stormwater networks, water/sewer reticulation hydraulics, pump-station hydraulic design, pipeline network design) | Captured by the **ACT Professional Engineers Registration Scheme** (civil/mechanical) — **including work done offshore for ACT projects** — *unless* done under the **direction & oversight of a registered professional engineer** or to a **prescriptive standard**. | Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT); ACT PE Scheme. |
| **Structural design / RCC reinforcement & steel detailing** (pump sheds, slab/mesh details for school works) | Same ACT PE Scheme (structural). Detailing/drafting is fine; the **engineering design must sit under a registered structural engineer** who takes responsibility. The "concrete mesh detail" school example must be signed by a registered engineer. | Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT). |
| **Mechanical / pump-station engineering** (pump selection, HIS-code work) | ACT PE Scheme (mechanical) — same direction-&-oversight pathway. | Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT). |
| **Electrical design** | ACT PE Scheme now includes **electrical** engineering. Note: electrical *installation* is separately licensed Australia-wide, but Watmov isn't installing. Electrical *design* must sit under a registered electrical engineer for ACT projects. | Professional Engineers Act 2023 (ACT); Electricity Safety Act 1971 (ACT) (installation). |
| **Specifying plumbing products / fixtures in designs** | Products on the **WaterMark Schedule** must be WaterMark-certified to be installed; WELS applies to water-using fixtures. Watmov doesn't certify products, but **specifications must call up WaterMark/WELS-compliant products** so the client isn't put offside. | NCC Vol 3 (PCA); WaterMark Certification Scheme (ABCB); WELS Act 2005. |

#### 🔴 RED / PROBLEMATIC — Do **not** offer as Watmov's own responsibility

| Activity | Why |
|---|---|
| **Issuing the certification / compliance declaration itself** (plumbing plan certification, engineer's compliance declaration, certifying As-Executed compliance) | These are **licensed/registered acts** reserved to Australian-licensed individuals (ACT Plumbing Plan Certifier; registered professional engineer). An offshore entity cannot hold these licences. Watmov must always position as **"prepared for certification by the licensed Australian principal."** |
| **Holding out / implying Watmov is a registered Australian engineer or certifier** | The ACT PE Act creates offences for **false/misleading representation about registration** and for **providing professional engineering services without registration** (where no exemption applies). Marketing must avoid implying registered status. |
| **Any physical plumbing/drainage/electrical *installation* work** | Licensed trades only (not part of Watmov's model anyway). |

### B.3 Cross-Jurisdiction Quick Reference (beyond ACT)

Watmov serves Australia broadly, so note where the *responsible* engineer's registration burden bites for the same work:

| Jurisdiction | Engineer-registration trigger relevant to Watmov's civil/hydraulic/structural/mech/elec work |
|---|---|
| **ACT** | Mandatory registration scheme (civil, structural, mechanical, electrical, fire safety) for services in **or for** the ACT, since 6 Mar 2025. Exemptions: direction & oversight, prescriptive standard. |
| **QLD** | **RPEQ** required for professional engineering services carried out **"in Queensland or for Queensland"** — explicitly captures interstate/overseas work for QLD projects. Exemptions: direct supervision by an RPEQ, or work to a prescriptive standard. *(Professional Engineers Act 2002 (Qld); BPEQ.)* |
| **VIC** | Registration required in 5 prescribed areas. Exemptions: direct supervision by a registered engineer, or prescriptive standard. Also note **VIC** regulates **non-agricultural irrigation design** as plumbing work under the Plumbing Regulations 2018 (VBA) — relevant if Watmov supports VIC irrigation. *(VBA.)* |
| **NSW** | Registration under the **Design and Building Practitioners Act 2020** is **narrow** — it bites mainly on **Class 2/3/9c (apartment-type) buildings** and "regulated designs." Most of Watmov's open-space irrigation, sports-field, road-median, pond, farm and bulk-water work is **outside** that scope. *(NSW Fair Trading; DBP Act 2020.)* |
| **Other states/NT** | Largely no general engineer registration; standard "responsible Australian principal certifies/submits" model applies. |

> **Practical takeaway:** The same drawing Watmov produces can be GREEN in NSW (open-space civil) but AMBER in ACT/QLD (registered engineer must own it). **Route all engineering design through a registered Australian professional who provides direction & oversight** — this single discipline keeps Watmov compliant across every state and unlocks the offshore model legally.

---

### B.4 Recommended Operating Guardrails for Watmov

1. **Always draft "for certification/approval by others."** Title blocks and proposals should state the Australian licensed principal certifies and submits. Avoid "For Construction" stamps issued by Watmov.
2. **Make the responsible-person sign-off explicit in scope.** For any civil/hydraulic/structural/mechanical/electrical design destined for ACT or QLD, confirm in the proposal that a **registered Australian engineer (the client or their nominee) provides direction & oversight and takes responsibility** — this invokes the legal exemption.
3. **Lean on the prescriptive-standard exemption.** Obtain and design to ACT **MITS 09D / MIS 18 (Irrigation)**, AS/NZS 3500, council standard drawings, and the TCCS specs Gavin offered — both improves quality *and* strengthens the exemption.
4. **Plumbing/sewer plans = draft only.** Deliver AS/NZS 3500-compliant plumbing/drainage plans to the client's **ACT Plumbing Plan Certifier** (Gavin's existing certifier, or himself once accredited). Never certify.
5. **Specifications must call up WaterMark/WELS-compliant products** so installs aren't rejected.
6. **Marketing/legal hygiene:** never represent Watmov (or its India engineers) as ACT-registered engineers or licensed certifiers. Position as drafting/design *support*.

---

### B.5 One-Line Summary

> **Irrigation, landscape, drafting, BoQ, conversions, sheet sets, and standards-driven design = no Australian certification needed (GREEN).** **All engineering design (civil/hydraulic/structural/mechanical/electrical) and plumbing/sewer plans = fully deliverable by Watmov but must be signed/certified by a registered/licensed Australian principal who provides direction & oversight (AMBER).** **Watmov must never itself certify, declare compliance, or hold out as Australian-registered (RED).** The ACT's 2024–25 engineer-registration scheme — which explicitly reaches offshore work done *for* ACT projects — makes the "responsible Australian engineer signs off" discipline non-negotiable for the EnvitoACT relationship.

## PART C — PLAIN-ENGLISH SUMMARY (ALL AUSTRALIAN STATES)

A simple "traffic light" version for quick reference across every state/territory.

### ✅ OK Everywhere — No certification, just do it
| What | Why it's fine |
|---|---|
| CAD drafting & detailing (AutoCAD, Civil 3D, Revit) | Drafting isn't a licensed job anywhere in Australia |
| PDF→CAD conversion, Google Earth/KMZ basing, survey-file → plan | Pure production work |
| Bills of Quantities / material take-offs | No certification exists for this |
| As-built / Works-As-Executed drawings from client data | The licensed installer signs the record, not Watmov |
| Irrigation design (sprinkler, drip, ovals, parks, medians, landscape) | No legal licence to design irrigation; the "Certified Irrigation Designer" badge is **voluntary** |
| Sheet sets, title blocks, specification documents | Documentation, not certification |
| Anything drawn strictly to a published standard (council standard drawings, AS/NZS 3500 layouts, ACT MITS/MIS) | "Prescriptive standard" = exempt |

### ⚠️ RISKY — Watmov can do the work, but an Australian licensed/registered person MUST sign off
| What | The catch | Worst states |
|---|---|---|
| Plumbing / sewer / sanitary-drainage plans | Must be **certified by a licensed plumbing plan certifier** & drawn to AS/NZS 3500 — Watmov drafts, the Aussie certifies | ACT (strict), most states |
| Civil & hydraulic engineering (stormwater networks, water/sewer hydraulics, pump-station hydraulics, pipeline networks) | A **registered engineer must take responsibility** | **ACT & QLD** (capture offshore work done *for* their projects), VIC |
| Structural design / RCC reinforcement & steel detailing (pump sheds, slab/mesh) | Must sit under a **registered structural engineer** | ACT, QLD, VIC |
| Mechanical / pump engineering | Must sit under a **registered mechanical engineer** | ACT, QLD, VIC |
| Electrical design | Must sit under a **registered electrical engineer** (separate from install licences) | ACT, QLD, VIC |
| Specifying plumbing fixtures/products | Specs must call up **WaterMark/WELS-compliant** products | All states |

> **The golden rule that makes all of the above safe:** route every engineering design through a **registered Australian engineer (the client) who gives "direction & oversight" and takes responsibility.** That single habit keeps Watmov legal in every state.

### 🚫 BIG NO-NO — Never, anywhere
| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Issue the certification / compliance declaration itself (plumbing plan cert, engineer's sign-off) | Reserved for Australian-licensed individuals — an offshore firm can't hold these |
| Claim or imply Watmov / its India engineers are Australian-registered engineers or certifiers | It's a **legal offence** (esp. ACT) to misrepresent registration |
| Do physical plumbing / electrical / drainage installation | Licensed trades only (not Watmov's business anyway) |

### State cheat-sheet
- **ACT & QLD** = strictest — their rules reach work done *offshore for* their projects → always need a registered engineer behind the design.
- **VIC** = registration in 5 engineering areas; also treats non-agricultural irrigation design as plumbing work.
- **NSW** = relaxed for Watmov's typical open-space/sports/civil work (its engineer rules mainly hit apartment-type Class 2/3/9c buildings).
- **Other states / NT** = no general engineer registration; just "the responsible Australian signs and submits."

**One-liner:** Drafting, irrigation & standards-based work = green light. All real engineering & plumbing plans = fine to produce, but an Australian must sign. Never certify or claim to be registered yourself.
